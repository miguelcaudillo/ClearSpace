# ClearSpace — Google Business Profile Photo Shot List

Google favors profiles with lots of **real** photos (stock hurts you). Aim for **10–15** to start, then add a few after every job. Since your supplies are already staged for the verification video, grab most of these in the same session.

## Ground rules
- **Landscape** orientation, phone camera, good natural light (near a window or outside).
- **Real, not stock.** Slightly imperfect real photos beat polished stock every time.
- Keep customers' faces, house numbers, and personal items out of frame (privacy).
- Shoot a few extra of each — you'll pick the best.

## The must-have shots (in priority order)

**1. Logo** — for the "Logo" slot.
- Your ✦ ClearSpace logo on a clean background. Screenshot it from the website header, or photograph a business card / shirt with the logo. Square-ish crops best.

**2. Cover photo** — the big banner; make it your strongest image.
- A bright, clean, finished room (your own home works) — a sparkling kitchen or living room. Wide landscape shot.

**3–4. Equipment / supplies** (2 shots)
- Your cleaning caddy, sprays, and cloths neatly arranged on a counter.
- Vacuum + mop + bucket lined up, ready to go.

**5. You / the "team"** (1–2 shots)
- You holding your caddy or standing with your supplies, friendly and approachable. If you have a branded shirt, wear it. A real face builds trust fast for a new business.

**6–9. Before & after** (2 pairs = 4 photos) — your highest-converting content
- **Kitchen:** one messy/greasy "before," one gleaming "after" from the same angle.
- **Bathroom:** one "before" (soap scum, water spots), one "after" (shining glass, fixtures).
- *Tip:* shoot the "before" the moment you arrive at a job (with the customer's OK), then the "after" from the exact same spot.

**10. Work vehicle** (if you have one)
- Parked, clean, ideally with supplies visible in the back or a magnet/sign if you have one.

**11–12. Detail / "proof of quality" shots**
- A close-up of a polished faucet, streak-free glass, or vacuum lines in carpet.
- Your printed room-by-room checklist (print `docs/checklists.html`) on a counter — signals your process.

## After the shoot
- Upload in GBP under **Edit profile → Photos**, assigning Logo and Cover first, then adding the rest to "By owner."
- **Re-upload a couple fresh ones every 1–2 weeks** — Google rewards active profiles, and recent before/afters from real jobs are the best marketing you have.
- Ask happy customers if you can photograph their finished rooms — with permission, those become your strongest ongoing content.

# ClearSpace — First Week of Operations Checklist

A practical playbook for your first jobs. Everything here ties back to tools you already
have in this folder. Work top to bottom; check the boxes as you go.

---

## Day 0 — Before your very first job

**Supplies kit (pack this once, restock after each job):**
- ☐ Vacuum (with hose + attachments), microfiber mop + bucket
- ☐ Microfiber cloths — lots (color-code: one color for bathrooms, another for kitchen — never cross them)
- ☐ All-purpose cleaner, glass cleaner, bathroom/tub cleaner, disinfectant, degreaser
- ☐ Scrub pads, old toothbrush (grout/corners), scraper, gloves
- ☐ Trash bags, paper towels, a step stool
- ☐ Phone charged for before/after photos
- ☐ Printed **`docs/checklists.html`** (one per job) + a pen

**Admin:**
- ☐ Confirm you're **licensed & insured** before touching a paying customer's home. This is the one non-negotiable — an accident without insurance can end the business. (Utah business license is quick and cheap; general liability insurance for cleaners runs ~$40–60/mo.)
- ☐ Save every new customer using **`docs/intake-form.html`** (print a stack, or fill on your phone). Pets, gate codes, product preferences, off-limits areas — get these BEFORE you arrive.
- ☐ Decide how you'll take payment: Venmo / Zelle / Cash App / cash. Set it up in advance.

---

## Pricing your first jobs

- ☐ Use your own calculator: **miguelcaudillo.github.io/ClearSpace/pricing.html** — quote every job from it so pricing stays consistent.
- ☐ **Always start a new customer with a Deep Clean** (or move-out), never a standard. A first-time home hasn't been professionally cleaned, and a standard-clean price won't cover the hours it really takes. Quote the deep, then offer recurring at the discounted rate going forward.
- ☐ **Honest first-week strategy:** it's fine to under-earn slightly on your first 3–5 jobs to build reviews and references. Consider a small "grand opening" discount (e.g. 15% off the first clean) IN EXCHANGE for a Google review and permission to photograph your work. Reviews are worth more than the discount.
- ☐ Send the quote with **`tools/quote-engine.js`** wording (run `node tools/quote-engine.js` to see the templates), or copy from the app. Same message every time = professional.
- ☐ Don't forget to factor **drive time and supply cost** into whether a far or tiny job is worth it.

## Day-of job flow (repeat every job)

1. ☐ **Text the day before** (reminder template is in `tools/quote-engine.js`).
2. ☐ **Arrive on time**, dressed clean, friendly. First impression = whether they rebook.
3. ☐ **Walkthrough with the customer first** — confirm priorities, off-limits areas, and any surprises. If the home is much worse than quoted, say so kindly BEFORE you start and agree on any change, never after.
4. ☐ **Take "before" photos** (with permission) — kitchen + bathrooms especially.
5. ☐ **Work the printed checklist** top to bottom. Work one room fully before moving on. Top-down, left-to-right, floors last.
6. ☐ **Final walkthrough** — walk the customer (or yourself) through room by room. Fix anything now.
7. ☐ **Take "after" photos** from the same spots as the before shots.
8. ☐ **Collect payment**, thank them warmly, and **ask if they'd like to set up recurring** while you're standing there (easiest yes you'll ever get).

---

## After each job (same day — don't skip)

- ☐ **Send the review request** that evening (SMS template in `tools/quote-engine.js`). Timing matters — ask while the clean home is still fresh in their mind. *This is the single most important habit of your first month.*
- ☐ Log the client + job in the office app (**app.html**) so you have a record and can invoice.
- ☐ Restock your supply kit for tomorrow.
- ☐ Save your best before/after photos to a folder — you'll post these and add them to the website.

---

## Getting your first reviews (your #1 early priority)

- ☐ You need **5+ Google reviews** before the profile really starts pulling in strangers. Get there fast: ask every single early customer, in person AND by text.
- ☐ Make it one tap — your review link goes in the texts/emails (send it to me once GBP is verified and I'll wire it in).
- ☐ If someone hesitates or seems less than thrilled, ask "what would have made it a 5-star clean?" — fix it free within 48 hours. A recovered customer becomes your most loyal one.
- ☐ **Never buy or fake reviews.** Google detects it and can suspend your profile — and it's dishonest. Real reviews from real first customers are the whole game.

---

## Money & admin basics (set up in week 1, thank yourself in April)

- ☐ **Separate the money:** open a free business checking account (or at minimum a dedicated account) so business and personal don't mix. Makes taxes 10× easier.
- ☐ **Track every dollar in and out** from day one — income per job, and expenses (supplies, gas, insurance, this website's future domain). A simple spreadsheet is fine to start.
- ☐ **Log your mileage** to jobs — it's a real tax deduction for a service business.
- ☐ **Set aside ~25–30% of profit for taxes** in a separate spot so it's not a shock. (You're self-employed now — no one's withholding for you.)
- ☐ Keep receipts for all supplies and equipment — deductible.

---

## End-of-week review (do this every Sunday)

- ☐ How many jobs? How much earned? How many reviews collected?
- ☐ Which quote turned into a booking, and which didn't — why?
- ☐ Post one before/after to Facebook/Instagram/Nextdoor (`marketing/social-media-kit.md`).
- ☐ Refill supplies for next week.
- ☐ Follow up with anyone who got a quote but didn't book (the GHL Day-1/Day-3 texts in `marketing/ghl-followup-sequence.md` — you can send these by hand until GoHighLevel is set up).

---

*You've got the website, pricing, docs, and marketing all built. The only thing left is the work itself — show up, follow the checklist, ask for the review. That's the whole business.*
